# Hardware-Reverse-Engineering
Exploring the hardware architecture and harvesting sensors from a Microsoft Optical Mouse for AI robotics integration
Project: As an AI Engineer and Computer Science student, I am exploring the intersection of hardware and software. This project involves the systematic teardown and reverse engineering of a Microsoft Optical Mouse (Model: X01-03410-02) to harvest high-quality components for future robotics and AI-powered computer vision projects.The goal is to move beyond "black box" consumer electronics and understand the embedded systems that facilitate human-computer interaction.üõ† Hardware Analysis & Component Inventory1. The Optical Sensor (The "Brain")Identification: High-speed CMOS image sensor located at the center of the PCB.Function: Acts as a low-resolution camera that captures thousands of images per second to calculate displacement vectors (X and Y coordinates).Robotics Application: Planned use as an Optical Flow Sensor for autonomous navigation, allowing a robot to track its position relative to the floor without GPS.2. Micro-Switches (Mechanical Inputs)Identification: Two primary tactile switches located under the Left/Right click triggers.Function: Standard "Normally Open" (NO) momentary switches.Robotics Application: These will be harvested and repurposed as Bumper/Collision Sensors. When the robot physicaly contacts an obstacle, these switches will trigger an interrupt in the control logic (Python/C++) to initiate obstacle avoidance.3. Rotary Encoder (The Scroll Wheel)Identification: Infrared (IR) emitter and receiver pair used with the scroll wheel.Robotics Application: Repurposing this as a Tachometer to measure wheel RPM, ensuring the robot maintains a constant velocity.4.
